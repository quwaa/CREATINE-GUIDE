The Science

What Is Creatine?

Creatine is a naturally occurring compound stored primarily inside your muscles.

Its primary role is to regenerate ATP — your body's fastest energy source during explosive exercise.

More ATP means greater strength, better performance, and improved recovery between sets.

The Mechanism

How Creatine Works

During intense exercise, ATP is broken down into ADP. Creatine phosphate donates a phosphate group to ADP, rapidly regenerating ATP and extending your power output.

This cycle is what makes creatine uniquely effective for high-intensity, short-duration efforts.

Stay Hydrated

Optimal Hydration

Creatine Draws Water Into Muscle Cells

Not out of your body. This is a common misconception. The water moves intracellularly — into the muscle tissue where it supports cell volumisation and an anabolic environment.

You do not need to drink extraordinary amounts of water. Simply maintain good hydration because proper hydration always supports performance and recovery regardless of supplementation.

Aim for adequate daily intake based on your body weight and training intensity. Darker urine is your signal to drink more.

Expectations

Results Timeline

Week 1
Initial Response
Intracellular water uptake begins. You may notice a slight increase in body weight (0.5–1kg). This is water in muscle cells — normal and expected.
Week 2
Muscle Saturation Builds
Creatine stores continue accumulating. You may begin to notice improved endurance within sets and slightly shorter recovery between efforts.
Week 3

Most users report clearer strength improvements. More reps, heavier weights, faster recovery between sets. The effects become measurable in training logs.
Week 4
Full Saturation
Muscle creatine stores are fully saturated. You are now operating at maximum benefit from supplementation. Maintain with 3–5g daily.
Week 8+
Compounding Gains
The real benefits emerge here. Greater training volume consistently applied over weeks compounds into meaningful strength and muscle growth beyond what creatine alone achieves.

Safety

Side Effects

Normal
  • Slight weight gain (water in muscle cells)
  • No change in urine colour with good hydration
  • Fuller, slightly harder muscles
  • All expected and harmless
Minor Digestive Issues
  • Bloating if taken on an empty stomach
  • Mild GI discomfort at high loading doses
  • Solution: split doses, take with food or switch to smaller daily dose
Consult Your Doctor If…
  • You have pre-existing kidney disease
  • You are on prescription medication
  • You experience unusual or persistent symptoms
  • You have a relevant medical history
